## 🕐 Best Times of Day (EST)

### 🔥 Phase 1 — Pre-Market Prep (8:30–9:29 AM)

**Not for trading — for preparation.**

- Mark your key support and resistance levels on the 15min chart
- Use this time to identify levels and create a plan — never blindly guess when the session opens
- Note the direction of the trend on the Daily and 1H chart
- **No trades yet — just observe and plan**

### 🔥 Phase 2 — NY Open (9:30–11:00 AM) ← **PRIMARY WINDOW**

Riley's **#1 trading window**:

- Highest volume of the day = key levels get tested and respected cleanly
- Trends establish clearly in the first 90 minutes
- The market rises most aggressively during the first two hours after the open (9:30–11:30 AM EST)
- Reversals at key levels during this window have the strongest follow-through

**What to look for 9:30–11:00 AM:**

- ✅ Price approaching your pre-marked key level
- ✅ Trend clearly established (higher highs or lower lows)
- ✅ Reversal or retest candle forming AT the key level
- ✅ Enter on confirmation — not anticipation

### ⚠️ Phase 3 — Late Morning (11:00 AM–12:00 PM)

- Still tradeable but volume is dropping
- Only take setups if they are **extremely clean** at major levels
- Riley's strategy requires trend clarity — choppy action here reduces edge

### ❌ Phase 4 — Lunch Hour (12:00–2:00 PM) — **AVOID**

- Low volume = key levels get faked out constantly
- Trends reverse for no reason then reverse again
- This is where Riley's strategy gets the most false signals — **skip it entirely**

### ✅ Phase 5 — NY Afternoon (2:00–4:00 PM) ← **SECONDARY WINDOW**

- Institutional volume returns after lunch
- Trends often resume or confirm in the final 2 hours
- Best for **continuation trades** if the morning trend was strong
- 3:00–4:00 PM EST sees surges in activity — ideal for closing positions or late trend moves

## 🚫 Days to Completely Avoid

- **CPI release days** — key levels become meaningless
- **FOMC meeting days** — wild unpredictable moves
- **NFP Fridays** — trend breaks violently then reverses
- **Monday after a big gap open** — no clean trend yet

## 💡 Riley's Core Timing Philosophy

His strategy is built on one principle: **daily analysis of key support and resistance levels** — the goal is to develop a **sniper-like intuition** for spotting high-probability setups rather than blindly following trade alerts.

**Patience is the strategy:** wait for price to come to **YOUR** level, not chase it.

> The best trade of the day almost always happens in the **first 90 minutes**. If nothing clean sets up by 11 AM — there is no trade today.
